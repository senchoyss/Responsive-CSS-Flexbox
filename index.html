Grizzly

Grizzly is the name of one or more American subspecies of the brown bear. It is distributed mainly in Alaska (including adjacent islands) and in western Canada. A small population of these animals has been preserved in the continental United States in Montana, in the Yellowstone area and in northwestern Washington.

Lynx

Lynx, common lynx (Lynx lynx), a mammal of the feline family, the genus lynx. Length up to 130 cm, height at the withers up to 70 cm, weight 18-25 (rarely up to 30) kg. The paws are large, wide, and in winter they are covered with dense fur, which allows you to walk in the snow. The head is large, with a short muzzle. There are tassels at the ends of the ears; adult lynxes have "sideburns" on their cheeks.

Foxes

Foxes (Vulpes), a genus of mammals in the Canidae family. Body length is 40-90 cm; tail is long (up to 60 cm), fluffy. The head has an elongated muzzle, pointed ears. The limbs are relatively short. The hairline is high, thick and soft or rather sparse and coarse; the color ranges from red to silver-brown, almost black; occasionally pure white (albinos) are found or black individuals (melanists).
